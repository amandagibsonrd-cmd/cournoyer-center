Founder / Owner / Music Director / Drum Instructor

Rob Cournoyer

Born and raised in Houma, Rob Cournoyer built a professional music career that took him from South Louisiana to Berklee College of Music, New York City, Los Angeles, Las Vegas, and back home again. After decades of performing, recording, touring, and teaching, Rob returned to Houma to open Cournoyer Center for Music Performance.

Rob’s background includes formal training at Berklee College of Music, professional instruction at Cournoyer Center, School of Rock Las Vegas, Musicians Institute in Hollywood, and years of private drum teaching. His performing and recording career includes work with Sin City Sinners, Human Drama, Sound of the Blue Heart, Raging Slab, XanaX 25, and media music projects for film, television, trailers, radio, and commercials.

Amos Rodriguez

Amos Rodriguez is a guitar instructor at Cournoyer Center who helps students build a stronger connection to their instrument through patient, practical guidance. His teaching style is calm, encouraging, and focused on helping students feel comfortable as they learn new skills.

In lessons, Amos supports students as they develop technique, rhythm, chord knowledge, and musical confidence. He is especially helpful for students who benefit from clear steps, steady encouragement, and a teacher who makes the learning process feel approachable.

Guitar and Bass Instructor / Assistant Music Director

Nick Rhodes, Jr.

Nick Rhodes has carved out a unique and enduring presence in the music world, beginning his journey on bass and guitar at the young age of 13. He is known for his work with bands including Agents of Oblivion and Deadboy and the Elephantmen, along with significant collaborations with Dax Riggs, where Nick was sometimes credited under the moniker Rikki Rhodes.

Beyond those projects, Nick’s musical journey includes a rich history of playing alongside his father, Nikki Rhodes. That partnership helped shape his artistic voice. From writing records between the ages of 18 and 27 to sharing the stage with family and other bands in the area for more than 28 years, Nick’s experience reflects deep commitment to his craft.

As an instructor, Nick is known for his patient approach and his strong belief that music brings not only happiness, but can also act as a guide through life’s challenges. He helps students build practical skills while also reminding them that music can become a lifelong source of expression and resilience.

Bass / Guitar / Performing Musician

Mark Kryvanick

Mark began studying music at St. Bernadette grammar school. Mentored by Mike Loupe, Mark was reading both treble and bass clefs at a young age while playing saxophone and bass guitar. At Vandebilt Catholic High School, Mark played bass guitar in the pit for marching band and sousaphone and trombone in the concert band.

After graduating high school, Mark entered the University of New Orleans, majoring in Jazz Performance. He studied Jazz Harmony with Ellis Marsalis and Harold Battiste and Jazz Improvisation with Terence Blanchard. His private instructors while at UNO included Chris Severin and Bill Huntington. Mark was also the upright bassist in Terence Blanchard’s highly regarded UNO Jazz Combo.

Mark later moved to Lafayette to teach at Acadiana Center for the Arts and School of Rock. Wanting to do more performing, he relocated back to New Orleans in 2014 and became active in the city’s live music scene. He has played with groups and artists including Smoke N Bones, Greg Hicks and Friends, Haciendas Brass Band, NOLA Dukes, and George Porter, Jr.

Mark is a respected, knowledgeable performing musician who breaks down information in easy-to-understand steps, helping students feel at ease and confident as they learn.

Vocal Instructor / Department Head

Isabella Siddon

Isabella Siddon is a skilled vocalist and performer who enjoys sharing her knowledge and skills with students. She has always had a love and passion for music and singing. As a child, Isabella sang joyfully at church, where her mother noticed her passion and talent and enrolled her in voice lessons. She also participated in dance classes and choirs, which led her to a love of passion, musical theatre.

Isabella became involved in musical theatre productions at Sola, Stage, Thibodaux Playhouse, Vandebilt Catholic High School, and Louisiana State University. She has been involved in school choirs, honor choirs, LSU choirs, and the Honors Performance Society Choir that performed at Carnegie Hall, gaining skills such as reading music, sight singing, aural skills, figured bass, and more.

Isabella is currently an Ogden Honors College student studying for her Bachelor’s in Vocal Music and conducting music research under Dr. Lori Bade at LSU. She is the LSU Musical Theatre Club Secretary and has participated in many university musical theatre productions. She has experience in LSU’s opera and choir as well. She is a member of Sigma Alpha Iota Music Fraternity.

With honors music theory and aural skills classes, piano, voice, music history, and a wide range of performance experience, Isabella brings both skill and warmth to her students. She is knowledgeable, natural, and happy to share her passion for the arts with others in her community.

Owner / Music Director / Drum Instructor

Rob Cournoyer

Rob Cournoyer is the owner, music director, and drum instructor at Cournoyer Center for Music Performance. Born and raised in Houma, Rob began performing in the tri-parish area as a young musician before attending Berklee College of Music in Boston, where he earned a Bachelor of Music degree in Professional Music and Performance.

Rob’s career has included years of teaching and performing in New York City, Los Angeles, and Las Vegas. His teaching background includes private instruction, School of Rock Las Vegas, Musicians Institute in Hollywood, and Cournoyer Center. His professional performing and recording experience includes Sin City Sinners, Sound of the Blue Heart, Human Drama, Raging Slab, XanaX 25, and media music work for film, television, trailers, and commercials.

After decades away from Houma, Rob returned home to create a music performance school where students can receive quality instruction, learn from working musicians, and experience the confidence that comes from playing with others.

Vocal, Guitar, and Piano Instructor

Chelsea Dupre

Chelsea Dupre is an accomplished vocalist who loves sharing her knowledge, love, and passion for music with others. In her early years, she was surrounded by music because of her family, namely her father and maternal grandmother. She picked up drumming as soon as she could flail her arms as a toddler.

Through her grade school years, Chelsea picked up voice, guitar, and piano, practicing during band and choir rehearsals and other extra activities. She developed sight-reading and sight-singing skills and was able to read both treble and bass clefs by the age of ten.

Her tenure at Nicholls State University deepened her love for music and teaching. She worked under Kenneth Klaus and Valerie Francis in the Vocal Music Performance Department through several performances, workshops, master classes, and music theory classes.

Chelsea is able to create a fun and positive learning environment for students. She loves motivating students by setting short-term and long-term goals, and she continues to share music and the love of it with her community in the best ways she knows how.
